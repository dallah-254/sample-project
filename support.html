Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions

How do I install my new LED headlights?

Installation varies by vehicle model, but generally follows these steps:

  1. Disconnect the vehicle's battery
  2. Remove the existing headlight assembly
  3. Connect the wiring harness (plug-and-play for most models)
  4. Secure the new headlight assembly
  5. Test the lights before reassembling

For vehicle-specific guides, check our installation video library.

Do I need special tools for installation?

Most installations only require basic hand tools:

  • Screwdriver set (Phillips and flathead)
  • Socket wrench set
  • Trim removal tools (for some vehicles)
  • Electrical tape and wire connectors

All our products come with basic installation hardware included.

What does your warranty cover?

Our comprehensive warranty covers:

  • Manufacturing defects - 5 years
  • LED failure - 3 years
  • Water damage resistance - 2 years
  • Wiring harness issues - 2 years

Normal wear and tear, improper installation, or modifications are not covered.

How do I file a warranty claim?

To file a warranty claim:

  1. Visit our Warranty Center
  2. Have your order number and product details ready
  3. Upload photos/videos of the issue
  4. Submit the form and wait for approval (usually 24-48 hours)

Or contact our warranty department at warranty@lighthub.com

What is your return policy?

We offer a 30-day return policy on all unopened products. For opened items:

  • 30 days for full refund (minus 15% restocking fee)
  • 60 days for store credit
  • 90 days for exchange only

Products must be in original packaging with all accessories.

How long do returns take to process?

Return processing timeline:

  • Inspection: 2-3 business days after receipt
  • Refund processing: 3-5 business days
  • Store credit: 1-2 business days
  • Exchange shipping: 5-7 business days
My lights are flickering. What should I do?

Flickering usually indicates one of these issues:

  1. Loose connection: Check all wiring connections
  2. Voltage fluctuation: Test your vehicle's alternator
  3. Compatibility issue: You may need a CANbus adapter
  4. Faulty driver: Could be a defective LED driver

Try our troubleshooting wizard for personalized help.

Do I need error cancellers for my vehicle?

Some European vehicles (BMW, Mercedes, Audi, VW) and certain American vehicles may require error cancellers due to their sensitive CANbus systems. Check our compatibility guide or use our compatibility checker tool below.

Will these lights fit my 2023 Ford F-150?

To ensure compatibility:

  1. Check the product page for vehicle fitment guide
  2. Use our compatibility checker tool
  3. Verify your vehicle's trim level and package options
  4. Check if you have factory LED or halogen lights

Most F-150 models from 2015-2023 are compatible with our plug-and-play systems, but Adaptive Cruise Control and other sensors may affect fitment.
